# Gold Vein Privacy Policy Draft

Last updated: June 2026

Gold Vein is a discipleship adventure app that guides participants through Scripture-supported
real-world trails.

This draft is a starting point for review before public app-store submission.

## Information Gold Vein Uses

### Location

Gold Vein may ask for location permission when a participant chooses to confirm that they are at an
adventure place.

Location is used to unlock the next step of an adventure.

In the current V1 prototype, location is processed on the participant's device and is not sent to a
server.

### Field Notes and Witness Notes

Gold Vein allows participants to write field notes, witness notes, and testimony text.

In the current V1 prototype, these notes are saved locally in the participant's browser or app
storage. They are not sent to a server unless a future version adds a submission feature and the
participant chooses to submit them.

### Redemption Codes

Gold Vein may display or redeem adventure passes, such as a coffee pass.

In the current V1 prototype, redemption status is stored locally on the device. A future version may
use a server to validate one-time codes across devices.

## How Information Is Used

Gold Vein uses information to:

- guide participants through adventure steps
- unlock adventure progress
- save local progress
- prepare field notes or testimony
- support pass redemption in pilot adventures

## Information Sharing

The current V1 prototype does not sell personal information.

The current V1 prototype does not send participant notes, location, or redemption data to a server.

Future versions that include accounts, testimony submission, or backend redemption should update
this policy before launch.

## Data Storage

The current V1 prototype stores progress and notes locally on the participant's device.

Participants can reset trail progress inside the app.

## Children's Privacy

Gold Vein is not currently designed for unsupervised use by children.

If Gold Vein is used with minors in a church, family, or group setting, adult supervision and local
church policies should be followed.
